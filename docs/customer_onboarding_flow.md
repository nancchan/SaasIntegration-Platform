# Customer Onboarding Workflow

## Overview

This document describes a typical SaaS customer onboarding workflow and how customer information moves between business systems.

The purpose is to understand how support and implementation teams monitor customer data throughout the onboarding process.

---

# Customer Journey

## 1. Customer Information Collection

A customer provides information during signup.

Example information:

- Customer name
- Email address
- Company name
- Contact information


↓

## 2. Data Validation

Customer information is reviewed to identify:

- Missing required fields
- Incorrect formatting
- Duplicate records


↓

## 3. Data Preparation

Customer data is cleaned and prepared before being imported into CRM systems.

Examples:

- Standardizing company names
- Correcting email formatting
- Removing duplicate records


↓

## 4. Salesforce CRM

Customer information is mapped into Salesforce objects.

Examples:

- Lead
- Account
- Contact
- Opportunity


↓

## 5. Reporting and Support

Teams use CRM records and reports to track customer activity and resolve issues.

---

# Common Onboarding Issues

## Data Quality Issues

Examples:

- Missing email addresses
- Invalid customer information
- Duplicate records


## CRM Issues

Examples:

- Incorrect field mapping
- Missing CRM records
- Permission issues


## Reporting Issues

Examples:

- Incorrect record counts
- Missing customer information


---

# Support Perspective

When onboarding issues occur, support teams investigate where the customer data flow stopped and identify the cause before applying a solution.
